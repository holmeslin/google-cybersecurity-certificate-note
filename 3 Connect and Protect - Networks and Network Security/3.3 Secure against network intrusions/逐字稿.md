### How intrusions compromise your system 入侵如何危害你的系統

In this section of the course, you learned that every network has inherent vulnerabilities and could become the target of a network attack.
在本課程的這一部分，你了解到每個網路都存在固有的漏洞，並可能成為網路攻擊的目標。

攻擊者攻擊你組織的網路可能有不同的動機。

They may have financial, personal, or political motivations, or they may be a disgruntled employee or an activist who disagrees with the company's values and wants to harm an organization’s operations.
他們可能出於經濟、個人或政治動機，或者他們可能是對公司價值觀不滿並想損害組織運作的員工或激進分子。

Malicious actors can target any network.
惡意行為者可以針對任何網路。

Security analysts must be constantly alert to potential vulnerabilities in their organization’s network and take quick action to mitigate them.
安全分析師必須時刻警惕組織網路中潛在的漏洞，並迅速採取行動來減輕這些漏洞。

在這篇閱讀中，你將了解網路攔截攻擊和後門攻擊，以及這些攻擊可能對組織造成的影響。

**Network interception attacks**
**網路攔截攻擊**

Network interception attacks work by intercepting network traffic and stealing valuable information or interfering with the transmission in some way.
網路攔截攻擊透過攔截網路流量並竊取有價值的資訊或以某種方式干擾傳輸來進行。

Malicious actors can use hardware or software tools to capture and inspect data in transit.
惡意行為者可以使用硬體或軟體工具來捕獲和檢查傳輸中的資料。

This is referred to as packet sniffing.
這稱為封包嗅探。

In addition to seeing information that they are not entitled to, malicious actors can also intercept network traffic and alter it.
除了看到他們無權看到的資訊外，惡意行為者還可以攔截網路流量並對其進行更改。

These attacks can cause damage to an organization’s network by inserting malicious code modifications or altering the message and interrupting network operations.
這些攻擊可能會透過插入惡意程式碼修改或更改訊息並中斷網路運作來損害組織的網路。

For example, an attacker can intercept a bank transfer and change the account receiving the funds to one that the attacker controls.
例如，攻擊者可以攔截銀行轉帳，並將接收資金的帳戶更改為攻擊者控制的帳戶。

Later in this course you will learn more about malicious packet sniffing, and other types of network interception attacks: on-path attacks and replay attacks.
在本課程的稍後部分，你將了解更多有關惡意封包嗅探以及其他類型的網路攔截攻擊：中間人攻擊和重播攻擊。

**Backdoor attacks**
**後門攻擊**

A backdoor attack is another type of attack you will need to be aware of as a security analyst.
後門攻擊是作為安全分析師需要注意的另一種攻擊。

An organization may have a lot of security measures in place, including cameras, biometric scans and access codes to keep employees from entering and exiting without being seen.
組織可能採取了很多安全措施，包括攝影機、生物識別掃描和存取代碼，以防止員工在未被發現的情況下進出。

However, an employee might work around the security measures by finding a backdoor to the building that is not as heavily monitored, allowing them to sneak out for the afternoon without being seen.
然而，員工可能會透過找到建築物中一個不太受監控的後門來繞過安全措施，使他們可以在不被發現的情況下溜出去一下午。

In cybersecurity, backdoors are weaknesses intentionally left by programmers or system and network administrators that bypass normal access control mechanisms.
在網路安全中，後門是程式設計師或系統和網路管理員故意留下的弱點，它們繞過了正常的存取控制機制。

Backdoors are intended to help programmers conduct troubleshooting or administrative tasks.
後門旨在幫助程式設計師進行故障排除或管理任務。

However, backdoors can also be installed by attackers after they’ve compromised an organization to ensure they have persistent access.
但是，後門也可能在攻擊者入侵組織後安裝，以確保他們擁有持續的存取權限。

Once the hacker has entered an insecure network through a backdoor, they can cause extensive damage: installing malware, performing a denial of service (DoS) attack, stealing private information or changing other security settings that leaves the system vulnerable to other attacks.
一旦駭客透過後門進入不安全的網路，他們可能會造成廣泛的損害：安裝惡意軟體、執行阻斷服務 (DoS) 攻擊、竊取私人資訊或更改其他安全設定，使系統容易受到其他攻擊。

A DoS attack is an attack that targets a network or server and floods it with network traffic.
DoS 攻擊是一種針對網路或伺服器並用網路流量淹沒它的攻擊。

**Possible impacts on an organization**
**對組織的可能影響**

As you’ve learned already, network attacks can have a significant negative impact on an organization.
正如你已經學到的，網路攻擊可能會對組織產生重大的負面影響。

Let’s examine some potential consequences.
讓我們來檢視一些潛在的後果。

-   Financial: When a system is taken offline with a DoS attack or some other tactic, they prevent a company from performing tasks that generate revenue.
    財務：當系統因 DoS 攻擊或其他策略而離線時，它們會阻止公司執行產生收入的任務。

Depending on the size of an organization, interrupted operations can cost millions of dollars.
根據組織的規模，中斷的營運可能會造成數百萬美元的損失。

Reparation costs to rebuild software infrastructure and to pay large sums associated with potential ransomware can be financially difficult.
重建軟體基礎架構的修復成本以及與潛在的勒索軟體相關的大筆付款在財務上可能很困難。

此外，如果惡意行為者存取了公司客戶或顧客的個人資訊，如果客戶尋求法律途徑，公司可能面臨巨額的訴訟和和解費用。

-   Reputation: Attacks can also have a negative impact on the reputation of an organization.
    聲譽：攻擊也可能對組織的聲譽產生負面影響。

If it becomes public knowledge that a company has experienced a cyber attack, the public may become concerned about the security practices of the organization.
如果一家公司遭受網路攻擊的消息公諸於眾，公眾可能會開始擔心該組織的安全措施。

They may stop trusting the company with their personal information and choose a competitor to fulfill their needs.
他們可能不再信任該公司處理他們的個人資訊，而選擇競爭對手來滿足他們的需求。

-   Public safety: If an attack occurs on a government network, this can potentially impact the safety and welfare of the citizens of a country.
    公共安全：如果政府網路受到攻擊，這可能會影響一個國家公民的安全和福祉。

近年來，全球的國防機構都在大力投資打擊網路戰策略。

If a malicious actor gained access to a power grid, a public water system, or even a military defense communication system, the public could face physical harm due to a network intrusion attack.
如果惡意行為者存取了電網、公共供水系統，甚至是軍事國防通訊系統，公眾可能會因為網路入侵攻擊而面臨人身傷害。

## Secure networks against Denial of Service (DoS) attacks 保護網路免受阻斷服務 (DoS) 攻擊

### Denial of Service (DoS) attacks 阻斷服務 (DoS) 攻擊

Welcome back.
歡迎回來。

In this video, we're going to discuss denial of service attacks.
在這部影片中，我們將討論阻斷服務攻擊。

A denial of service attack is an attack that targets a network or server and floods it with network traffic.
阻斷服務攻擊是一種針對網路或伺服器並用網路流量淹沒它的攻擊。

The objective of a denial of service attack, or a DoS attack, is to disrupt normal business operations by overloading an organization's network.
阻斷服務攻擊或 DoS 攻擊的目的是透過使組織的網路過載來中斷正常的業務運作。

The goal of the attack is to send so much information to a network device that it crashes or is unable to respond to legitimate users.
攻擊的目標是向網路裝置發送大量資訊，使其崩潰或無法回應合法的用戶。

This means that the organization won't be able to conduct their normal business operations, which can cost them money and time.
這意味著組織將無法進行正常的業務運作，這可能會使他們損失金錢和時間。

A network crash can also leave them vulnerable to other security threats and attacks.
網路崩潰也可能使他們容易受到其他安全威脅和攻擊。

A distributed denial of service attack, or DDoS, is a kind of DoS attack that uses multiple devices or servers in different locations to flood the target network with unwanted traffic.
分散式阻斷服務攻擊，或 DDoS，是一種 DoS 攻擊，它使用不同位置的多個裝置或伺服器來用不需要的流量淹沒目標網路。

Use of numerous devices makes it more likely that the total amount of traffic sent will overwhelm the target server.
使用大量裝置會使發送的總流量更有可能使目標伺服器過載。

Remember, DoS stands for denial of service.
請記住，DoS 代表阻斷服務。

So it doesn't matter what part of the network the attacker overloads; if they overload anything, they win.
因此，攻擊者使網路的哪個部分過載並不重要；如果他們使任何東西過載，他們就贏了。

An unfortunate example I've seen is an attacker who crafted a very careful packet that caused a router to spend extra time processing the request.
我看過一個不幸的例子是，一名攻擊者精心製作了一個封包，導致路由器花費額外的時間來處理請求。

The overall traffic volume didn't overload the router; the specifics within the packet did.
整體流量並未使路由器過載；封包內的特定內容使其過載。

Now we'll discuss network level DoS attacks that target network bandwidth to slow traffic.
現在我們將討論針對網路頻寬以減緩流量的網路層級 DoS 攻擊。

Let's learn about three common network level DoS attacks.
讓我們來了解三種常見的網路層級 DoS 攻擊。

The first is called a SYN flood attack.
第一個稱為 SYN 洪水攻擊。

A SYN flood attack is a type of DoS attack that simulates the TCP connection and floods the server with SYN packets.
SYN 洪水攻擊是一種模擬 TCP 連線並用 SYN 封包淹沒伺服器的 DoS 攻擊。

Let's break this definition down a bit more by taking a closer look at the handshake process that is used to establish a TCP connection between a device and a server.
讓我們透過仔細查看用於在裝置和伺服器之間建立 TCP 連線的交握過程來更詳細地分解此定義。

The first step in the handshake is for the device to send a SYN, or synchronize, request to the server.
交握的第一步是裝置向伺服器發送一個 SYN 或同步請求。

Then, the server responds with a SYN/ACK packet to acknowledge the receipt of the device's request and leaves a port open for the final step of the handshake.
然後，伺服器以 SYN/ACK 封包回應，以確認收到裝置的請求，並為交握的最後一步保留一個開啟的埠。

Once the server receives the final ACK packet from the device, a TCP connection is established.
一旦伺服器收到來自裝置的最終 ACK 封包，就會建立 TCP 連線。

Malicious actors can take advantage of the protocol by flooding a server with SYN packet requests for the first part of the handshake.
惡意行為者可以透過用 SYN 封包請求淹沒伺服器來利用該協定進行攻擊，這些請求僅針對交握的第一部分。

But if the number of SYN requests is larger than the number of available ports on the server, then the server will be overwhelmed and become unable to function.
但是，如果 SYN 請求的數量大於伺服器上可用埠的數量，則伺服器將不堪重負並變得無法運作。

Let's discuss two other common DoS attacks that use another protocol called ICMP.
讓我們討論另外兩種使用另一種稱為 ICMP 的協定的常見 DoS 攻擊。

ICMP stands for Internet Control Message Protocol.
ICMP 代表網際網路控制訊息協定。

ICMP is an internet protocol used by devices to tell each other about data transmission errors across the network.
ICMP 是一種由裝置使用的網際網路協定，用於相互告知網路上的資料傳輸錯誤。

Think of ICMP like a request for a status update from a device.
將 ICMP 視為來自裝置的狀態更新請求。

The device will return error messages if there is a network concern.
如果存在網路問題，裝置將傳回錯誤訊息。

You can think of this like the ICMP request checking in with the device to make sure that all is well.
你可以將此視為 ICMP 請求與裝置進行檢查，以確保一切正常。

An ICMP flood attack is a type of DoS attack performed by an attacker repeatedly sending ICMP packets to a network server.
ICMP 洪水攻擊是一種由攻擊者重複向網路伺服器發送 ICMP 封包而執行的 DoS 攻擊。

This forces the server to send an ICMP packet.
這會強制伺服器發送一個 ICMP 封包。

This eventually uses up all the bandwidth for incoming and outgoing traffic and causes the server to crash.
這最終會耗盡所有傳入和傳出流量的頻寬，並導致伺服器崩潰。

Both of the attacks we've discussed so far, SYN flood and ICMP flood, take advantage of communication protocols by sending an overwhelming number of requests.
到目前為止，我們討論的兩種攻擊，SYN 洪水和 ICMP 洪水，都是透過發送大量請求來利用通訊協定。

There are also attacks that can overwhelm the server with one big request.
還有一些攻擊可以透過一個大型請求使伺服器不堪重負。

One example that we'll discuss is called the ping of death.
我們將討論的一個例子稱為死亡之 Ping。

A ping of death attack is a type of DoS attack that is caused when a hacker pings a system by sending it an oversized ICMP packet that is bigger than 64 kilobytes, the maximum size for a correctly formed ICMP packet.
死亡之 Ping 攻擊是一種 DoS 攻擊，當駭客透過向系統發送一個大於 64 KB 的超大 ICMP 封包來 Ping 系統時，就會發生這種攻擊，64 KB 是格式正確的 ICMP 封包的最大大小。

Pinging a vulnerable network server with an oversized ICMP packet will overload the system and cause it to crash.
使用超大的 ICMP 封包 Ping 易受攻擊的網路伺服器會使系統過載並導致其崩潰。

Think of this like dropping a rock on a small anthill.
可以將此想像成將一塊石頭丟在一個小蟻丘上。

Each individual ant can carry a certain amount of weight while transporting food to and from the anthill.
每隻螞蟻在來回蟻丘搬運食物時，都可以承受一定的重量。

But if a large rock is dropped on the anthill, then many ants will be crushed, and the colony is unable to function until it rebuilds its operations elsewhere.
但是，如果一塊大石頭掉在蟻丘上，那麼許多螞蟻將被壓死，蟻群將無法運作，直到它在其他地方重建其運作。

Now that's it for DoS and DDoS attacks.
現在 DoS 和 DDoS 攻擊就到此為止。

目標是在資料封包中找到有價值的資訊，然後他們可以利用這些資訊來謀取利益。

Attackers can use software applications or a hardware device to look into data packets.
攻擊者可以使用軟體應用程式或硬體裝置來查看資料封包。

Malicious actors can access a network packet with a packet sniffer and make changes to the data.
惡意行為者可以使用封包嗅探器存取網路封包並更改資料。

They may change the information in the body of the packet, like altering a recipient's bank account number.
他們可能會更改封包主體中的資訊，例如更改收件人的銀行帳號。

Packet sniffing can be passive or active.
封包嗅探可以是被動的或主動的。

Passive packet sniffing is a type of attack where data packets are read in transit.
被動封包嗅探是一種在傳輸過程中讀取資料封包的攻擊類型。

Since all the traffic on a network is visible to any host on the hub, malicious actors can view all the information going in and out of the device they are targeting.
由於網路上所有流量對於集線器上的任何主機都是可見的，因此惡意行為者可以查看進出他們目標裝置的所有資訊。

Thinking back to the example of a letter being delivered, we can compare a passive packet sniffing attack to a postal delivery person maliciously reading somebody's mail.
回想一下信件遞送的例子，我們可以將被動封包嗅探攻擊比喻為郵務人員惡意閱讀別人的信件。

The postal worker, or packet sniffer, has the right to deliver the mail, but not the right to read the information inside.
郵務人員或封包嗅探器有權遞送信件，但無權閱讀裡面的資訊。

Active packet sniffing is a type of attack where data packets are manipulated in transit.
主動封包嗅探是一種在傳輸過程中操縱資料封包的攻擊類型。

This may include injecting internet protocols to redirect the packets to an unintended port or changing the information the packet contains.
這可能包括注入網際網路協定以將封包重新導向到非預期的埠，或更改封包包含的資訊。

Active packet sniffing attack would be like a neighbor telling the delivery person "I'll deliver that mail for you," and then reading the mail or changing the letter before putting it in your mailbox.
主動封包嗅探攻擊就像鄰居告訴送貨員「我來幫你送這封信」，然後在把信放到你家信箱之前閱讀信件或更改信件內容。

Even though your neighbor knows you and even if they deliver it to the correct house, they are actively going out of their way to engage in malicious behavior.
即使你的鄰居認識你，即使他們把它送到正確的房子，他們也在積極地以自己的方式從事惡意行為。

The good news is that malicious packet sniffing can be prevented.
好消息是，可以預防惡意封包嗅探。

Let's look at a few ways the network security professional can prevent these attacks.
讓我們看看網路安全專業人員可以預防這些攻擊的幾種方法。

One way to protect against malicious packet sniffing is to use a VPN to encrypt and protect data as it travels across the network.
一種防止惡意封包嗅探的方法是使用 VPN 來加密和保護資料在網路上傳輸。

If you don't remember how VPNs work, you can revisit the video about this topic in the previous section of the program.
如果你不記得 VPN 如何運作，你可以回顧本課程前一節中關於此主題的影片。

When you use a VPN, hackers might interfere with your traffic, but they won't be able to decode it to read it and read your private information.
當你使用 VPN 時，駭客可能會干擾你的流量，但他們將無法解碼它來閱讀它並閱讀你的私人資訊。

Another way to add a layer of protection against packet sniffing is to make sure that websites you have use HTTPS at the beginning of the domain address.
另一種增加針對封包嗅探的保護層的方法是確保你使用的網站在其網域位址的開頭使用 HTTPS。

Previously, we discussed how HTTPS uses SSL/TLS to encrypt data and prevent eavesdropping when malicious actors spy on network transmissions.
先前，我們討論了 HTTPS 如何使用 SSL/TLS 來加密資料並防止惡意行為者監視網路傳輸時進行竊聽。

One final way to help protect yourself against malicious packet sniffing is to avoid using unprotected WiFi.
最後一種幫助保護自己免受惡意封包嗅探的方法是避免使用未受保護的 WiFi。

You usually find unprotected WiFi in public places like coffee shops, restaurants, or airports.
你通常會在咖啡店、餐廳或機場等公共場所找到未受保護的 WiFi。

These networks don't use encryption.
這些網路不使用加密。

This means that anyone on the network can access all of the data traveling to and from your device.
這意味著網路上任何人都可以存取所有進出你裝置的資料。

One precaution you can take is avoiding free public WiFi unless you have a VPN service already installed on your device.
你可以採取的一項預防措施是避免使用免費的公共 WiFi，除非你的裝置上已安裝 VPN 服務。

Now you know how threat actors may use packet sniffing and how to protect a network from these attacks.
現在你了解了威脅行為者可能如何使用封包嗅探以及如何保護網路免受這些攻擊。

### IP Spoofing

IP 欺騙

Next, let's learn about another kind of network attack called IP spoofing.
接下來，讓我們學習另一種稱為 IP 欺騙的網路攻擊。

IP spoofing is a network attack performed when an attacker changes the source IP of a data packet to impersonate an authorized system and gain access to a network.
IP 欺騙是一種網路攻擊，當攻擊者更改資料封包的來源 IP 以冒充授權系統並存取網路時執行。

In this kind of attack, the hacker is pretending to be someone they are not so they can communicate over the network with the target computer and get past firewall rules that may prevent outside traffic.
在這種攻擊中，駭客假裝成他們不是的人，以便他們可以透過網路與目標電腦通訊，並繞過可能阻止外部流量的防火牆規則。

Some common IP spoofing attacks are on-path attacks, replay attacks, and smurf attacks.
一些常見的 IP 欺騙攻擊是中間人攻擊、重播攻擊和 smurf 攻擊。

Let's discuss these one at a time.
讓我們一次討論一個。

An on-path attack is an attack where the malicious actor places themselves in the middle of an authorized connection and intercepts or alters the data in transit.
中間人攻擊是一種攻擊，惡意行為者將自己置於授權連線的中間，並攔截或更改傳輸中的資料。

On-path attackers gain access to the network and put themselves between two devices, like a web browser and a web server.
中間人攻擊者存取網路並將自己置於兩個裝置之間，例如網路瀏覽器和網路伺服器。

Then they sniff the packet information to learn the IP and MAC addresses to devices that are communicating with each other.
然後他們嗅探封包資訊以了解彼此通訊的裝置的 IP 和 MAC 位址。

After they have this information, they can pretend to be either of these devices.
在他們獲得此資訊後，他們可以假裝成這些裝置中的任何一個。

Another type of attack is a replay attack.
另一種攻擊是重播攻擊。

A replay attack is a network attack performed when a malicious actor intercepts a data packet in transit and delays it or repeats it at another time.
重播攻擊是一種網路攻擊，當惡意行為者攔截傳輸中的資料封包，然後延遲或在稍後重複它時執行。

A delayed packet can cause connection issues between target computers, or a malicious actor may take a network transmission that was sent by an authorized user and repeat it at a later time to impersonate the authorized user.
延遲的封包可能會導致目標電腦之間的連線問題，或者惡意行為者可能會取得由授權使用者發送的網路傳輸，並在稍後重複它以冒充授權使用者。

A smurf attack is a combination of a DDoS attack and an IP spoofing attack.
smurf 攻擊是 DDoS 攻擊和 IP 欺騙攻擊的組合。

The attacker sniffs an authorized user's IP address and floods it with packets.
攻擊者嗅探授權使用者的 IP 位址，並用封包淹沒它。

This overwhelms the target computer and can bring down a server or the entire network.
這會使目標電腦不堪重負，並可能導致伺服器或整個網路癱瘓。

Now that you've learned about different kinds of IP spoofing, let's talk about how you can protect the network from this kind of attack.
既然你已經了解了不同類型的 IP 欺騙，讓我們來談談如何保護網路免受此類攻擊。

As you previously learned, encryption should always be implemented so that the data in your network transfers can't be read by malicious actors.
正如你先前學到的，應始終實施加密，以使惡意行為者無法讀取網路傳輸中的資料。

Firewalls can be configured to protect against IP spoofing.
可以配置防火牆以防禦 IP 欺騙。

IP spoofing makes it seem like the malicious actor is an authorized user by changing the sender's address of the data packet to match the target network's address.
IP 欺騙透過將資料封包的發送者位址更改為與目標網路的位址相符，使惡意行為者看起來像是授權使用者。

So if a firewall receives a data packet from the internet where the sender's IP address is the same as the private network, then the firewall will deny the transmission since all the devices with that IP address should already be on the local network.
因此，如果防火牆收到來自網際網路的資料封包，其中發送者的 IP 位址與私有網路相同，則防火牆將拒絕傳輸，因為具有該 IP 位址的所有裝置都應該已在區域網路上。

You can make sure that your firewalls configure correctly by creating a rule to reject all incoming traffic that has the same IP address as the local network.
你可以透過建立規則來拒絕所有具有與區域網路相同 IP 位址的傳入流量，以確保防火牆配置正確。

That's it for IP spoofing.
IP 欺騙就到此為止。

You've learned how IP spoofing is used in some common attacks like on-path attacks, replay attacks, and smurf attacks.
你已經了解了 IP 欺騙如何在一些常見的攻擊中使用，例如中間人攻擊、重播攻擊和 smurf 攻擊。

### Overview of interception tactics

攔截策略概述

In the previous course items, you learned how packet sniffing and IP spoofing are used in network attacks.
在先前的課程項目中，你學習了如何在網路攻擊中使用封包嗅探和 IP 欺騙。

Because these attacks intercept data packets as they travel across the network, they are called interception attacks.
由於這些攻擊會攔截在網路上傳輸的資料封包，因此它們被稱為攔截攻擊。

This reading will introduce you to some specific attacks that use packet sniffing and IP spoofing.
這篇閱讀將向你介紹一些使用封包嗅探和 IP 欺騙的特定攻擊。

You will learn how hackers use these tactics and how security analysts can counter the threat of interception attacks.
你將學習駭客如何使用這些策略，以及安全分析師如何應對攔截攻擊的威脅。

**A closer review of packet sniffing**
**更深入地回顧封包嗅探**

As you learned in a previous video, packet sniffing is the practice of capturing and inspecting data packets across a network.
正如你在先前的影片中學到的，封包嗅探是在網路上捕獲和檢查資料封包的實務。

On a private network, data packets are directed to the matching destination device on the network.
在私有網路上，資料封包會被導向網路上匹配的目的地裝置。

The device’s Network Interface Card (NIC) is a piece of hardware that connects the device to a network.
裝置的網路介面卡 (NIC) 是一種將裝置連接到網路的硬體。

The NIC reads the data transmission, and if it contains the device’s MAC address, it accepts the packet and sends it to the device to process the information based on the protocol.
NIC 會讀取資料傳輸，如果它包含裝置的 MAC 位址，則會接受封包並將其發送給裝置，以根據協定處理資訊。

This occurs in all standard network operations.
這發生在所有標準網路操作中。

However, a NIC can be set to promiscuous mode, which means that it accepts all traffic on the network, even the packets that aren’t addressed to the NIC’s device.
但是，NIC 可以設定為混雜模式，這意味著它會接受網路上的所有流量，即使是未發送到 NIC 裝置的封包。

You’ll learn more about NIC’s later in the program.
你將在本課程的稍後部分了解更多有關 NIC 的資訊。

Malicious actors might use software like Wireshark to capture the data on a private network and store it for later use.
惡意行為者可能會使用像 Wireshark 這樣的軟體來捕獲私有網路上的資料並儲存以供日後使用。

They can then use the personal information to their own advantage.
然後他們可以利用個人資訊來謀取私利。

Alternatively, they might use the IP and MAC addresses of authorized users of the private network to perform IP spoofing.
或者，他們可能會使用私有網路授權使用者的 IP 和 MAC 位址來執行 IP 欺騙。

**A closer review of IP spoofing**
**更深入地回顧 IP 欺騙**

After a malicious actor has sniffed packets on the network, they can impersonate the IP and MAC addresses of authorized devices to perform an IP spoofing attack.
在惡意行為者嗅探網路上的封包後，他們可以冒充授權裝置的 IP 和 MAC 位址來執行 IP 欺騙攻擊。

Firewalls can prevent IP spoofing attacks by configuring it to refuse unauthorized IP packets and suspicious traffic.
可以透過將防火牆配置為拒絕未經授權的 IP 封包和可疑流量來防止 IP 欺騙攻擊。

Next, you’ll examine a few common IP spoofing attacks that are important to be familiar with as a security analyst.
接下來，你將檢查一些作為安全分析師必須熟悉的常見 IP 欺騙攻擊。

**On-path attack**
**中間人攻擊**

An on-path attack happens when a hacker intercepts the communication between two devices or servers that have a trusted relationship.
當駭客攔截兩個具有信任關係的裝置或伺服器之間的通訊時，就會發生中間人攻擊。

The transmission between these two trusted network devices could contain valuable information like usernames and passwords that the malicious actor can collect.
這兩個受信任的網路裝置之間的傳輸可能包含有價值的資訊，例如惡意行為者可以收集的使用者名稱和密碼。

An on-path attack is sometimes referred to as a meddler-in-the middle attack because the hacker is hiding in the middle of communications between two trusted parties.
中間人攻擊有時被稱為中間人攻擊，因為駭客隱藏在兩個受信任方之間的通訊中。

Or, it could be that the intercepted transmission contains a DNS system look-up.
或者，被攔截的傳輸可能包含 DNS 系統查詢。

You’ll recall from an earlier video that a DNS server translates website domain names into IP addresses.
你會從先前的影片中回想起，DNS 伺服器會將網站網域名稱轉換為 IP 位址。

If a malicious actor intercepts a transmission containing a DNS lookup, they could spoof the DNS response from the server and redirect a domain name to a different IP address, perhaps one that contains malicious code or other threats.
如果惡意行為者攔截了包含 DNS 查詢的傳輸，他們可能會欺騙來自伺服器的 DNS 回應，並將網域名稱重新導向到不同的 IP 位址，該位址可能包含惡意程式碼或其他威脅。

The most important way to protect against an on-path attack is to encrypt your data in transit, e.g.
保護免受中間人攻擊的最重要方法是加密傳輸中的資料，例如使用 TLS。

**Smurf attack**
**Smurf 攻擊**

A smurf attack is a network attack that is performed when an attacker sniffs an authorized user’s IP address and floods it with packets.
Smurf 攻擊是一種網路攻擊，當攻擊者嗅探授權使用者的 IP 位址並用封包淹沒它時執行。

Once the spoofed packet reaches the broadcast address, it is sent to all of the devices and servers on the network.
一旦欺騙的封包到達廣播位址，它就會被發送到網路上所有裝置和伺服器。

In a smurf attack, IP spoofing is combined with another denial of service (DoS) technique to flood the network with unwanted traffic.
在 smurf 攻擊中，IP 欺騙與另一種阻斷服務 (DoS) 技術結合使用，以用不需要的流量淹沒網路。

For example, the spoofed packet could include an Internet Control Message Protocol (ICMP) ping.
例如，欺騙的封包可能包含網際網路控制訊息協定 (ICMP) ping。

As you learned earlier, ICMP is used to troubleshoot a network.
正如你稍早學到的，ICMP 用於排除網路故障。

But if too many ICMP messages are transmitted, the ICMP echo responses overwhelm the servers on the network and they shut down.
但是，如果傳輸過多的 ICMP 訊息，ICMP 回應會使網路上的伺服器不堪重負，導致它們關閉。

This creates a denial of service and can bring an organization’s operations to a halt.
這會造成阻斷服務，並可能使組織的營運停頓。

An important way to protect against a smurf attack is to use an advanced firewall that can monitor any unusual traffic on the network.
防禦 smurf 攻擊的一個重要方法是使用可以監控網路上任何異常流量的進階防火牆。

Most next generation firewalls (NGFW) include features that detect network anomalies to ensure that oversized broadcasts are detected before they have a chance to bring down the network.
大多數新世代防火牆 (NGFW) 都包含偵測網路異常的功能，以確保在超大廣播有機會使網路癱瘓之前就偵測到它們。

**DoS attack**
**DoS 攻擊**

As you’ve learned, once the malicious actor has sniffed the network traffic, they can impersonate an authorized user.
正如你所學到的，一旦惡意行為者嗅探了網路流量，他們就可以冒充授權使用者。

A Denial of Service attack is a class of attacks where the attacker prevents the compromised system from performing legitimate activity or responding to legitimate traffic.
阻斷服務攻擊是一類攻擊，攻擊者會阻止受入侵的系統執行合法活動或回應合法流量。

Unlike IP spoofing, however, the attacker will not receive a response from the targeted host.
然而，與 IP 欺騙不同的是，攻擊者不會收到來自目標主機的回應。

Everything about the data packet is authorized including the IP address in the header of the packet.
資料封包的所有內容都是經過授權的，包括封包標頭中的 IP 位址。

In IP spoofing attacks, the malicious actor uses IP packets containing fake IP addresses.
在 IP 欺騙攻擊中，惡意行為者使用包含偽造 IP 位址的 IP 封包。

The attackers keep sending IP packets containing fake IP addresses until the network server crashes.
攻擊者不斷發送包含偽造 IP 位址的 IP 封包，直到網路伺服器崩潰。

Pro Tip: Remember the principle of defense-in-depth.
專家提示：請記住縱深防禦的原則。

There isn’t one perfect strategy for stopping each kind of attack.
沒有一種完美的策略可以阻止每種類型的攻擊。

You can layer your defense by using multiple strategies.
你可以透過使用多種策略來分層防禦。

In this case, using industry standard encryption will strengthen your security and help you defend from DoS attacks on more than one level.
在這種情況下，使用業界標準的加密將增強你的安全性，並幫助你從多個層面防禦 DoS 攻擊。

**Key takeaways**

你了解了如何執行封包嗅探，以及從攔截資料傳輸中收集資訊如何為惡意行為者提供 IP 欺騙的機會。

**主動封包嗅探：**一種在傳輸過程中操縱資料封包的攻擊類型

**Botnet:** A collection of computers infected by malware that are under the control of a single threat actor, known as the “bot-herder"
**殭屍網路：**受惡意軟體感染的電腦集合，受單個威脅行為者（稱為「殭屍網路主控者」）的控制

**Denial of service (DoS) attack:** An attack that targets a network or server and floods it with network traffic
**阻斷服務 (DoS) 攻擊：**一種針對網路或伺服器並用網路流量淹沒它的攻擊

**Distributed denial of service (DDoS) attack:** A type of denial of service attack that uses multiple devices or servers located in different locations to flood the target network with unwanted traffic
**分散式阻斷服務 (DDoS) 攻擊：**一種阻斷服務攻擊，它使用位於不同位置的多個裝置或伺服器來用不需要的流量淹沒目標網路

**Internet Control Message Protocol (ICMP):** An internet protocol used by devices to tell each other about data transmission errors across the network
**網際網路控制訊息協定 (ICMP)：**一種由裝置使用的網際網路協定，用於相互告知網路上的資料傳輸錯誤

**Internet Control Message Protocol (ICMP) flood:** A type of DoS attack performed by an attacker repeatedly sending ICMP request packets to a network server
**網際網路控制訊息協定 (ICMP) 洪水：**一種 DoS 攻擊，由攻擊者重複向網路伺服器發送 ICMP 請求封包來執行

**IP spoofing:** A network attack performed when an attacker changes the source IP of a data packet to impersonate an authorized system and gain access to a network
**IP 欺騙：**一種網路攻擊，當攻擊者更改資料封包的來源 IP 以冒充授權系統並存取網路時執行

**On-path attack:** An attack where a malicious actor places themselves in the middle of an authorized connection and intercepts or alters the data in transit
**中間人攻擊：**一種攻擊，惡意行為者將自己置於授權連線的中間，並攔截或更改傳輸中的資料

**Packet sniffing:** The practice of capturing and inspecting data packets across a network
**封包嗅探：**捕獲和檢查網路上資料封包的實務

**Passive packet sniffing:** A type of attack where a malicious actor connects to a network hub and looks at all traffic on the network
**被動封包嗅探：**一種攻擊類型，惡意行為者連接到網路集線器並查看網路上的所有流量

**Ping of death:** A type of DoS attack caused when a hacker pings a system by sending it an oversized ICMP packet that is bigger than 64KB
**死亡之 Ping：**一種 DoS 攻擊，當駭客透過向系統發送一個大於 64KB 的超大 ICMP 封包來 Ping 系統時，就會發生這種攻擊

**Replay attack:** A network attack performed when a malicious actor intercepts a data packet in transit and delays it or repeats it at another time
**重播攻擊：**一種網路攻擊，當惡意行為者攔截傳輸中的資料封包，然後延遲或在稍後重複它時執行

**Smurf attack:** A network attack performed when an attacker sniffs an authorized user’s IP address and floods it with ICMP packets
**Smurf 攻擊：**一種網路攻擊，當攻擊者嗅探授權使用者的 IP 位址並用 ICMP 封包淹沒它時執行

**Synchronize (SYN) flood attack:** A type of DoS attack that simulates a TCP/IP connection and floods a server with SYN packets
**同步 (SYN) 洪水攻擊：**一種 DoS 攻擊，它模擬 TCP/IP 連線並用 SYN 封包淹沒伺服器
